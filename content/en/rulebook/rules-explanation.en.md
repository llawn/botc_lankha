The [rules sheet](/en/sheets/rules/) describes all the major things that a new player will need to know to start playing.
Simply read out the text written on this *sheet* to the group, or let those who want to read it do so privately.

Part of the *rules sheet* will prompt you to talk about various hand signals.
You’ll want to demonstrate these hand signals as you go, since some players learn visually.

If you wish, you can read out the part of the *rules sheet* that talks about nominations and executions now,
but most players will learn this better if you read it out later on, when the first nomination for execution begins.

## Before the Game Starts

### Lore

**A long time ago**, in the vibrant city of **Lugdunum**, on a fog-shrouded night at the stroke of midnight... a scream echoed across the hills.

Drawn toward the heights of **Fourvière**, you rush to investigate.

There, before the great basilica, you discover a horrifying scene.

Your beloved Storyteller, **Lankha**, stands motionless, clutching a mysterious Grimoire.
The stained-glass windows of the basilica shimmer beneath the moonlight,
their reflections forming shapes that resemble the face of a giant clock.
The sight is strangely reminiscent of the illuminations that shine across Lyon during the Festival of Lights.

High upon the tower stands **Lankha**.

Below him spreads a vast pool of blood flowing from the Grimoire itself.
The crimson stream cascades down the ancient stonework, creeping across the square like a living tide.
Those unfortunate souls who wandered through the plaza at such a late hour now lie lifeless upon the ground,
their bodies carried by the slow-moving river of blood.

Something terrible has awakened.

By touching the Grimoire, **Lankha** unleashed a great upheaval upon Fourvière.
A curse now hangs over the city.
A bloodthirsty Demon has risen from its slumber, and strange powers begin to manifest among the surviving citizens, artisans, and wanderers who escaped the Demon’s first massacre.

As for **Lankha**, he no longer seems entirely human.

With every passing moment, he appears to transcend mortality itself, drawing ever closer to something divine... something eternal.

Tonight, the fate of *Lugdunum* rests in your hands.

### Goal

You are about to receive either a blue / dark-blue or a red / purple token.

If you receive a blue / dark-blue token, you are **good**.

If you receive a red / purple token, you are **evil**.

The goal of the good team is to identify and execute the Demon.
If the Demon dies, good wins.

The goal of the evil team is to bring ruin upon the city.
If only two players remain alive, evil wins.

### Day and Night Cycle

The game is divided into two phases: **Day** and **Night**.

During the day, players discuss, debate, accuse, and defend.
Each player has a secret identity represented by a character from the scenario being played.

Most good players will share information and work together to discover who can be trusted.
However, not everyone has a reason to tell the truth. Some characters benefit from deception.

If you are evil, you should almost certainly be lying.
A common strategy is to pretend to be a good character while spreading misinformation and confusion among the town.

### Night Actions

During the night, all players close their eyes.

Some players will be awakened individually so they may use their abilities or receive information.
While resolving night actions, **The Storyteller** will remain silent and communicate only through the following signals:

*( Demonstrate the following as you talk ).*
* Two taps on your shoulder or knee means: **OPEN YOUR EYES**.
* This gesture means **CLOSE YOUR EYES**. *Example: Hand on the forehead.*
* This gesture means **YES**, and this gesture means **NO**. *Example: Head movement or O/X with hands.*
* This gesture means **GOOD**, and this gesture means **EVIL**. *Example: Thumb up/down*
* These gestures indicate numbers: **0, 1, 2, 3**, and so on. *Example: Numbers of fingers, do not use your thumb alone*.
* To select a player at night, simply point to them.

### Death Is Not the End

Most of you will die. This is perfectly normal.

In BOTC, death is rarely the end of the story.
Dead players continue to participate in discussions, may still share information, and still win or lose alongside their team.

Some characters may even benefit from dying.

Never assume that a dead player is no longer important.

### Drunkenness and Poisoning

This game contains a great deal of information.

Not all of it is reliable.

If you become drunk or poisoned, your character's ability does not function correctly.
However, the *Storyteller* will continue to act as though it does.
Any information you receive may be completely false, and you will not be told whether you are drunk or poisoned.

For example, if you are [the Drunk](/en/characters/outsiders/drunk/), you may believe you are another character entirely.
Only the Storyteller knows your true identity.

Likewise, if a [Poisoner](/en/characters/minions/poisoner/) affects you,
you may still wake during the night and attempt to use your ability,
but the information you receive may be incorrect, or your ability may have no effect at all.

In this city, certainty is dangerous.

Trust carefully.

Question everything.

And remember:

[The Grimoire](/en/toolbox/) never lies.

But those who interpret its secrets often do.

##  Four Rules

This can be a lot of information to take in at once, so to keep things simple, there are only four things you need to remember:

1) **You may say whatever you want at any time.**
   This is a talking game. You can talk publicly with the group or have private conversations, text people it is up to you.

2) **No peeking.** Please keep your character a secret, and never look into [the Grimoire](/en/toolbox/),
   as it contains all the game characters. If you see something you shouldn’t, it will spoil the fun.

3) **Ask the Storyteller any questions you need to.**
   If you get confused, or don’t understand how your character works,
   or don’t understand how the character that you are pretending to be works,
   or if something happens at night that you don’t understand, or you just need some strategy advice...
   whatever it is, please ask.
   The Storyteller is neutral, and the job is to help you understand the rules and have fun playing.
   Let the Storyteller know when you have a question, and he can talk in private so that nobody knows what question you asked (like text messages).

4) **Play nice.** This is a game about deception and trickery, so please treat others with respect and consideration. Kill with grace, and die with dignity.

##  Before the First Nomination

To nominate a player, simply say who.
For example: “I nominate Bob.” When a player is nominated, everyone votes on whether or not to execute them.
The storyteller will put his arm out like this (point to Bob), and say “Votes for Bob, starting now.”
The he moves his hand in a clockwise direction (demonstrate this) and if your hand is up when I get to you, that’s a vote,
and if your hand is down, that’s not a vote.
You can vote last moment but be aware if it is too late and after the **Storyteller** pass through you, it is up to him to accept your vote.

Each day, you may vote for as few or as many players as you wish, and whoever has the most votes is executed.
With one exception rule: the player needs a vote of at least 50% of the living players or no execution occurs.
On a tie, neither player is executed.

If you die, you are still a major part of the game.
You still talk, and you still close your eyes during the night time.
Most importantly, you still win or lose with your team.
In fact, the game is usually decided by the votes and opinions of the dead players. When you die, you lose your character ability, you may no longer nominate, and you have only one vote for the rest of the game, so use it wisely.
