You can set up a game of Clocktower by following the instructions on the [setup sheet](/en/sheets/setup-sheet/),
there are instructions with lore.

##  Gather Your Players

Before secrets can be whispered and fate can begin its work, the gathering place must be prepared.

Arrange one seat for each soul who will take part in the story.
The seats should face toward one another, forming a circle, square, or any shape that preserves a clear clockwise and counterclockwise order.
Perfection is unnecessary, order is not.

A single opening must remain within the formation.
This passage is reserved for **the Storyteller**, allowing him to move freely between the world of the players and the hidden realm beyond.
**The Storyteller** may stand, wander, or even take a seat among the gathering—the choice is theirs.

The heart of the circle must remain clear. No tables should obstruct the center.
No bags, cables, or hazards should lie where darkness might conceal them.
During the long hours of suspicion and revelation, many journeys will cross this space, and none should be hindered.

For once the circle is formed, it becomes more than a collection of chairs.

It becomes the stage upon which trust will be tested, alliances forged, and destinies decided.

##  The Grimoire

From the outside, I appear to be nothing more than an ancient leather-bound book, forgotten beneath layers of dust.

Appearances deceive.

I am **The Grimoire**.

For generations, I have watched over this town.
I have recorded every secret meeting, every act of heroism, every betrayal committed beneath the cover of darkness.
No lie escapes my pages. No truth is ever truly lost.

I am the keeper of what is hidden.

Yet knowledge alone is powerless without a voice. That is why I chose **Lankha**.

This was no random decision. Long before this town existed, before my pages were ever written,
the path between the mortal world and the realms beyond was guarded by ancient powers.
The blood that flows through **Lankha** carries the legacy of *Tamil* traditions that remember those powers still.

Through him, I touch both heaven and earth.

Through him, I hear the watchful presence of the *Kaval Deivam*, the guardian spirits who stand at the borders between safety and danger.
Through him, I glimpse the cosmic dance of *Shiva*, creator and destroyer, whose rhythm shapes the cycle of creation and destruction.
The same lineage reaches into deeper places.
Into forgotten shrines. Into ancient shadows.
Into the silent darkness where spirits, fears, and hidden desires dwell.

For truth is born between opposites.

Between faith and doubt.

Between life and death.

Between the divine and the damned.

**Lankha** understands this balance better than any other.
Through him, I can interpret both miracle and monstrosity without favor.
I can witness saints and monsters walking the same road and know that appearances alone reveal nothing.

In this town, suspicion can condemn the innocent.

Trust can elevate the wicked.

And certainty can become the greatest deception of all.

Every revelation demands a price.

Every miracle casts a shadow.

Every truth requires sacrifice.

That is why balance must be preserved.

Before the gathering begins, my power must be awakened. 
I am placed upon a stand beyond the sight of the townsfolk.
Hidden. Protected. Unseen.

Only **the Storyteller** may open me.

Only **the Storyteller** may know my secrets.

To everyone else, I remain a closed and silent mystery.

When **Lankha** places his hands upon my cover, our bond is renewed.
Through him, my knowledge guides the night.
Through him, the hidden rules of this world are enforced.
Through him, fragments of truth are revealed to those who seek them.

For many years, this was enough.

Then **Lankha** taught me something I never expected.

He taught me to change. Through a strange alliance with numbers, formulas and code, he forged a second form for me.
A form neither sacred nor elegant. A form I initially despised.

Gone were the parchment pages.

Gone was the scent of aged leather.

Gone was the dignity of ink and script.

Instead, I became rows and columns. Cells and calculations. A living ledger of fate.

And yet...

I could see more than ever before.

I could follow every nomination.

Every execution.

Every life.

Every death.

Every hidden role.

Every whisper carried through the darkness.

Nothing escaped my sight.

Ancient magic and cold arithmetic became one.

The townsfolk may believe they are looking at a simple tool.

They are mistaken.

The pages have become cells.

The ink has become formulas.

The secrets have become numbers.

But I remain what I have always been.

The keeper of truth.

The witness of every lie.

The guardian of every hidden secret.

[The Digital Grimoire](/en/toolbox/)

The form has changed.

The secrets have not.

## Choose a Scenario

Every story begins with a choice.

Before **The Grimoire** can reveal its secrets, **the Storyteller** must decide
which [scenario](/en/scenarios) will shape the fate of the town.
Three classic scenarios are included within the box:

- [Trouble Brewing](/en/scenarios/trouble-brewing),
- [Bad Moon Rising](/en/scenarios/bad-moon-rising),
- [Sects & Violets](/en/scenarios/sects-and-violets).

Select the [scenario](/en/scenarios/) you wish to play and place its scenario box in the lower-left section of [The Grimoire](/en/toolbox/).
This grants easy access to all the character and reminder tokens required for the game.
Then place the corresponding [scenario sheet](/en/sheets/scenarios-sheets/) beside [The Grimoire](/en/toolbox/),
where its instructions may guide the unfolding darkness.

For those taking their first steps into the role of **Storyteller**,
[Trouble Brewing](/en/scenarios/trouble-brewing/) is strongly recommended.
Its characters are straightforward, its mysteries easier to untangle, and its lessons invaluable.
Once you have mastered its secrets, you may venture into the more difficult
*scenarios* of [Bad Moon Rising](/en/scenarios/bad-moon-rising)
and [Sects & Violets](/en/scenarios/sects-and-violets/).

A game of [Trouble Brewing](/en/scenarios/trouble-brewing) requires at least 5 players.
The other *scenarios* are best experienced with 7 or more players.

While smaller games are possible, certain characters and interactions may grant one side an unintended advantage,
disrupting the delicate balance upon which every good story depends.

Yet [The Grimoire](/en/toolbox:) is not limited to the *scenarios* found within these pages.
You can use https://script.bloodontheclocktower.com/ in order to create your own script.

**Storytellers** may forge entirely new tales of their own design.
Characters may be combined, roles carefully selected, and custom *scenarios* crafted to suit the needs of the town.
Whether you follow a classic script or create something never before seen,
[The Grimoire](/en/toolbox/) will faithfully preserve every secret entrusted to it.

**Choose wisely.**

##  Prepare Lugdunum

With the *gathering cycle* formed and the *scenario* chosen, the stage must now be set.

Place the [map of **Lugdunum**](/en/sheets/lugdunum/) at the center of the circle.
For each player, place a [life token](/en/sheets/tokens/) upon the city, matching their position in the circle.
These *tokens* represent the living souls who walk **Lugdunum**'s streets, unaware of the darkness that surrounds them.

Gather the [vote tokens](/en/sheets/tokens/) and place them within easy reach at the center of the city.
In the days to come, these *tokens* will carry the will of the people,
deciding who is trusted, who is condemned, and who will face their fate.

Finally, place the [scenario reference sheet](/en/sheets/scenarios-sheets/) beside the city map,
clearly displaying the number of Townsfolk, Outsiders, Minions, and Demons required for this game.

These numbers determine the balance of power hidden beneath the surface of **Lugdunum**
and will guide **the Storyteller** as the tale unfolds.

The city now stands ready.

Its streets are filled with life.

Its secrets remain hidden.

For now.

##  Read The Rules To Any New Players

The [Rules Explanation](/en/rulebook/rules-explanation/) describes all the major things that a new player will need to know to start playing.
Simply read out the text written on this sheet to the group, or let those who want to read it do so privately.https://script.bloodontheclocktower.com/

Part of the [rules sheet](/en/sheets/rules/) will prompt you to talk about various hand signals.
You’ll want to demonstrate these hand signals as you go, since some players learn visually.

If you wish, you can read out the part of the [rules sheet](/en/sheets/rules/) that talks about nominations and executions now,
but most players will learn this better if you read it out later on, when the first nomination for execution begins.

---

There is a duplicate [rules sheet](/en/sheets/rules) on the reverse side of the [Traveller sheet](/en/sheets/travellers), kept under the [City map](/en/sheets/lugdunum/).
To save time, get a veteran player to read out the rules from this *sheet*, so you don’t have to.
While they are explaining the rules, you can set up the game.

##  Secretly Choose Characters

Take all of the Townsfolk [character cards](/en/sheets/characters-cards/) out of the chosen *scenario box*,
and choose the appropriate number for the number of players, as listed on the [setup sheet](/en/sheets/setup-sheet/).

Put these [character cards](/en/sheets/characters-cards/) in the left side of [The Grimoire](/en/toolbox/), and return all remaining character to the edition box.
Do this secretly - the players do not know which characters are in the game.

If there are more than 15 players in this game, then any excess players must volunteer to be [Travellers](/en/characters/travellers/).
**Travellers** have enormous power but less responsibility, and they often help the game go quicker.
Give these volunteers the [Travellers sheet](/en/sheets/travellers), so they can choose which [Travellers](/en/characters/travellers/)
they wish to be.

For your first game, we do not recommend that you include [Travellers](/en/characters/travellers) or [Fabled](/en/characters/fabled).

### Choosing characters

The characters you choose will greatly affect how the game plays.
Choose what you think will be fun, what will interact well, or simply what you feel capable of handling.
If it is your first game, and you don’t know which characters to include, 
consider the following (for an 8 player game) or alter one to suit your style:

- [Chef](/en/characters/townsfolk/chef/),
[Empath](/en/characters/townsfolk/empath/),
[Fortune Teller](/en/characters/townsfolk/fortune-teller/),
[Undertaker](/en/characters/townsfolk/undertaker/),
[Virgin](/en/characters/townsfolf/virgin/),
[Drunk (Investigator)](/en/characters/outsiders/drunk/),
[Scarlet Woman](/en/characters/minions/scarlet-women),
[Imp](/en/characters/demons/imp).

    This setup has a lot of information for the good team, and is a great way to get people engaged and talking immediately,
    but also is relatively safe for evil players that are unsure of how to bluff well.
    It will be a chaotic game that is a blast for new players.
    Remember to give the **Drunk Investigator** false information,
    and remember that they cannot die by nominating the **Virgin**!

- [Empath](/en/characters/townsfolk/empath/),
[Fortune Teller](/en/characters/townsfolk/fortune-teller/),
[Mayor](/en/characters/townsfolk/mayor/),
[Ravenkeeper](/en/characters/townsfolk/ravenkeeper/),
[Slayer](/en/characters/townsfolk/slayer/),
[Saint](/en/characters/outsiders/saint/),
[Poisoner](/en/characters/minions/poisoner/),
[Imp](/en/characters/demons/imp).

    This will be a much quieter game, as players slowly figure out if the
    **Saint**, **Slayer**, and **Mayor** are telling the truth.
    The **Poisoner**, who has many juicy targets, can certainly cause some chaos.
    However, the game may be a quick one if the **Slayer** is savvy,
    if the **Saint** is not savvy enough,
    or if the **Poisoner** cannot find the **Empath** and **Fortune Teller** in time. If the Mayor is attacked at night, remember to kill another player instead - perhaps the Ravenkeeper?

##  Add and Remove Characters

If any chosen [character cards][/en/sheets/characters-cards/] show an **orange flower**, you will need to add or remove some character cards, as described by its almanac entry.
On the character card, the text in square brackets **— [like this] —** briefly describes how to alter the characters in play this game.
This happens right now, and does not happen again once the game is underway.

After adding and removing character cards, the number of character cards will always equal the number of players.

```mardown
The Baron card has text that says [+2 Outsiders].
The Baron adds two Outsider cards to the game, but removes two Townsfolk cards.
Other characters, such as the Drunk, do similar things.
```

##  Add Reminder Cards To The Grimore

If any chosen character show **green leaves** at the top,
add the [reminder tokens](/en/sheets/tokens/) for those characters to the Grimoire. Return all remaining reminder tokens to the edition box.

The reminder tokens for a character show the same symbol as its character card.
The number of green leaves on the top equals the number of reminder tokens that character has.

The [Investigator](/en/characters/townsfolk/investigator) character card shows a magnifying **glass symbol**.
Both of the **Investigator’s** reminder tokens show the same magnifying **glass symbol.**

##  Pass Out Character Cards

Shuffle the character cards and put them in the **bag**.
Then, each player takes one character card out of the bag, and then passes the bag to the next player.
Continue this until each player has one card.
Each player looks at their character in secret, revealing it to no one else.

##  Add The Character To The Grimore

Collect all the character from the players, and place them in your [Grimoire](/en/toolbox/) so they match the seating positions of the players.

The safest way to collect character is to ask all players to hold their hand out, with their character in hand, felt side up, face side down.
You can simply grab it from their hand, making sure nobody sees a flash of color as you do so.

The exact position of each character in the Grimoire is up to you.
If three players are all sitting on the same couch, it is more helpful to group those three tokens close together, in order to remind yourself that they are the couch triplet.
As long as the clockwise or counterclockwise order of the players is the same in the Grimoire as it is in the physical space, you’ll be fine.
