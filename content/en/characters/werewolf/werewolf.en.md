##  Summary
"Each night*, choose a player: they die. If you kill a lover, both die tonight."

The Werewolf stalks the village each night, driven by an insatiable hunger.
* Each night except the first, the Werewolf chooses a player to kill.
* The Werewolf knows which Minions are in play, and three not-in-play good characters to bluff as.
* If the Werewolf kills a player who is a lover, that player's lover also dies the same night.
* The Werewolf wins if only evil players remain alive.

##  How to Run

Each night except the first, wake the Werewolf. They point to any living player. That player dies. If that player is marked as a **LOVER**, the other lover also dies. Put the Werewolf to sleep.

##  Examples

{{< example >}}
The Werewolf kills Marie on night three. Marie is not a lover. Only Marie dies.
{{< /example >}}

{{< example >}}
The Werewolf kills Pierre. Pierre is a lover of Claire. Both Pierre and Claire die.
{{< /example >}}

{{< example >}}
The Werewolf kills Claire, who is Cupid's lover (Cupid made themselves Claire's lover). Both Claire and Cupid die.
{{< /example >}}

##  Tips & Tricks

* Target players who are trusted by the village. Removing a vocal, credible good player sows chaos.
* Pay attention to who seems protected. If the Guard is protecting someone, target someone else.
* Coordinate with Cupid. If you know who the lovers are, you can engineer a double kill.
* Bluff as a Townsfolk convincingly. Your three bluffs are your lifeline.
* Consider killing yourself if a Minion can take over. A fresh face as the Demon can confuse the village.

##  Bluffing as the Werewolf

As the Demon, you shouldn't need to bluff as the Werewolf. Instead, bluff as Townsfolk or Outsiders to hide your identity. If you're a Minion pretending to be the Werewolf (e.g., after a failed execution), claim that the village is confused about who the Demon is.
