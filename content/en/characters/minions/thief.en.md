##  Summary
"Once per game, at night, choose a player: you gain their ability and they lose theirs."

The Thief slips through shadows and steals more than just coin—they steal identities.
* Once per game, at night, the Thief chooses a living player.
* The Thief gains that player's ability, and that player loses their ability for the rest of the game.
* The Thief learns the character of the player they stole from.
* The Thief retains their own Minion abilities after stealing.
* If the Thief steals from a player with a spent ability, they gain nothing.

##  How to Run

Once per game, at night, wake the Thief. They point to a living player. Show the Thief that player's character token. That player loses their ability—mark them with the **NO ABILITY** reminder. The Thief gains that ability. Mark the Thief with the stolen character's reminder if applicable. Put the Thief to sleep.

##  Examples

{{< example >}}
The Thief steals from the Seer. The Thief now sees players' characters each night. The Seer learns nothing more.
{{< /example >}}

{{< example >}}
The Thief steals from the Guard on night one. The Guard can no longer protect anyone. The Thief has wasted their theft—the Guard's protection is most useful early.
{{< /example >}}
