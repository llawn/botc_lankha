##  Summary
"Each night, choose a player: they are safe from the Werewolf tonight. You cannot choose yourself."

The Guard stands watch through the night, protecting a chosen villager from harm.
* Each night, the Guard wakes and chooses a living player other than themselves.
* The chosen player cannot be killed by the Werewolf that night.
* The Guard's protection does not prevent death by execution, the Witch's potion, or the Hunter's revenge.
* The Guard may choose the same player on consecutive nights.

##  How to Run

Each night, wake the Guard. They point to any living player except themselves. Mark that player with the **PROTECTED** reminder. If the Werewolf chooses that player, the Werewolf's kill fails—do not kill the protected player. Remove the reminder at dawn.

##  Examples

{{< example >}}
The Guard protects Marie on night two. The Werewolf chooses Marie. Marie does not die.
{{< /example >}}

{{< example >}}
The Guard protects Pierre. Pierre is executed at dawn. The Guard's protection does not save Pierre from execution.
{{< /example >}}

##  Tips & Tricks

* Protect high-value targets like the Seer or Witch. Keep them alive to use their abilities.
* Don't always protect the same person. The Werewolf may try to predict your choice.
* You can't protect yourself, so stay hidden. Don't reveal your role too early or the Werewolf will target you.
* Pay attention to who the Werewolf kills each night. If they never target the person you're protecting, you may be wasting your protection.

##  Bluffing as the Guard

When bluffing as the Guard, names the players you "protected" each night. If a player you claim to have protected dies anyway, have an explanation ready (the Witch's potion, execution, etc.). If you're the Werewolf, claiming to be the Guard gives you a reason to be alive each morning.
