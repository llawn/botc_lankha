##  Summary
"If you would die, you may choose a player: they die instead."

The Hunter takes their killer with them to the grave.
* If the Hunter is executed or killed by the Werewolf, they may immediately choose a player to die.
* The Hunter does not act if they die during the day by execution and no nomination is in progress.
* The chosen player dies immediately, regardless of protection.
* If the Hunter is killed by the Witch's killing potion, the Hunter may still use their ability.

##  How to Run

If the Hunter dies, wake them immediately (even if it is during the day). They point to any player. That player dies. If the Hunter chooses not to use their ability, or if they are executed and choose not to act, put them to sleep without effect. If the Hunter is killed at night, wake them before putting them in the dead row.

##  Examples

				The Hunter is executed by the village. The Hunter points at Sophie. Sophie dies. Sophie was the Werewolf—the game ends.

				The Werewolf kills the Hunter at night. The Hunter points at Marc. Marc was the Cupid. Marc dies, and Marc's lover dies too.

##  Tips & Tricks

* Use your death to take out your biggest suspect. Even if you're wrong about an evil player, you remove a potential threat.
* If you have a strong suspicion about who the Werewolf is, let it be known before you die so your execution has purpose.
* Coordinate with the Seer or Witch to confirm a target before you go.
* You can also use your ability to kill a player you trust, if they want to die for strategic reasons (e.g., to gain information as a dead player).

##  Bluffing as the Hunter

Bluffing as the Hunter is relatively safe—evil players are unlikely to target you for fear of your revenge kill. If you're a Minion, claim to be the Hunter to deter the Werewolf from killing you. If you're the Werewolf, claiming to be the Hunter can explain why you're still alive after multiple nights.
