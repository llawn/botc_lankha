##  Summary
"Once per game, you may save a player from death. Once per game, you may poison a player."

The Witch holds two powerful potions: one to cheat death, one to deal it.
* The Witch has two separate one-use abilities: a healing potion and a killing potion.
* The healing potion may be used at any night after a player dies. That player is saved and does not die.
* The killing potion may be used at any night to poison a player. That player dies immediately.
* The Witch may use both potions in the same night, or on different nights.
* The Witch learns who was targeted by the Werewolf each night.

##  How to Run

Each night, wake the Witch. Show them who the Werewolf has chosen to kill (if the Werewolf is acting tonight). If the Witch has their healing potion, they may point to any dead player to save them—remove that player from the dead. If the Witch has their killing potion, they may point to any living player—that player dies. If the Witch uses a potion, mark them with the appropriate reminder. Put the Witch to sleep.

##  Examples

				Night one: the Werewolf kills Jean. The Witch learns Jean is dead and uses her healing potion. Jean lives.

				Night three: the Witch suspects Claire is evil. She uses her killing potion on Claire. Claire dies. Claire was the Thief.

##  Tips & Tricks

* Your healing potion is precious. Save it for a confirmed good player or someone with crucial information.
* Your killing potion can remove a suspected evil player without wasting a daytime execution.
* Learning who the Werewolf targeted each night gives you valuable information about who the Werewolf considers a threat.
* Coordinate with the Seer to confirm targets before using your killing potion.

##  Bluffing as the Witch

When bluffing as the Witch, you need to explain when you used your potions and on whom. Claim to have saved a player who survived for unrelated reasons, or claim to have poisoned a player who died for other reasons. Be prepared for the Storyteller to contradict you.
